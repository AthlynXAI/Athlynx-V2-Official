## V. What We Are NOT (The "Do Not" List — Locked)

We will **never** do the following in AthlynXAI marketing, advertising, social, sales, or investor communications:

1. **Never lead with features.** "213+ platform features" is dairy-industry marketing. We do not sell milk by the gallon.
2. **Never compare ourselves to competitors by spec sheet.** "Faster than X, cheaper than Y." That game is a race to zero.
3. **Never sell "AI-powered" as the value.** AI is the engine under the hood; nobody buys a car to admire the carburetor.
4. **Never sell on tokens, credits, speeds, or megahertz.** That is the language of the commodity. We are not a commodity.
5. **Never market to the platform; always market to the person.** We do not address "users." We address athletes, coaches, parents, scouts, agents.
6. **Never let the brand voice forget the journey.** If a piece of copy could run for any other AI company, it is not AthlynXAI copy.
7. **Never claim numbers we cannot prove.** Truth is our marketing. Inflated user counts, fabricated dashboards, "vanity metrics" — banned. Forever. (See *Manus precedent.*)
8. **Never use emojis in core brand voice.** Founder's standing rule.

## VII. The Three Reference Points (Founder's Examples)

These three examples are the canonical case studies for AthlynXAI marketing strategy. Every marketer, every agent, every employee should be able to recite all three:

### 1. The Dairy Industry — *How NOT to Market*
For 20 years the dairy industry sold milk on facts: "Milk does a body good." Calcium. Vitamins. Bone density. Speeds-and-feeds marketing applied to a beverage. Sales collapsed. Lesson: **specs alone do not move people. Meaning does.**

### 2. "Got Milk?" — *Marketing the Feeling, Not the Product*
The campaign that turned dairy around did not show milk's benefits. It showed the *absence* of milk at the exact moment you needed it most — a mouth full of peanut butter, an empty carton, panic. Lesson: **sell the feeling around the product, not the product itself.**

### 3. Nike — *The Identity Brand Standard*
Nike sells rubber and canvas. A commodity. And yet Nike is one of the most valuable brands in the world because Nike does not sell shoes — **Nike honors athletes.** That is the founder's North Star for AthlynXAI marketing. We do not sell a social/NIL/AI/calendar platform. We honor the athlete's journey.

## IX. The Test (Before Anything Ships)

Before any AthlynXAI marketing asset goes out the door — ad, page, post, deck, video, email — it must pass three questions:

1. **Does it honor the journey?** If no, kill it.
2. **Would Nike be proud of it?** If no, rewrite it.
3. **Would Mama recognize her son in it?** If no, start over.

That third question is the final gate. The founder's mother just wrote, on Mother's Day, that he is her hero and that God walked them through the dark. **Nothing ships under this brand that the founder's mother would not be proud to read aloud at the dinner table.**
