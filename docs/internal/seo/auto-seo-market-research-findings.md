# Auto SEO Market Research Findings

## Source: NCSA — Social Media for College Recruiting

URL: https://www.ncsasports.org/recruiting/contacting-college-coaches/social-media

Key findings captured for AthlynXAI Auto SEO:

- Social media is increasingly important in college recruiting because coaches use Instagram, TikTok, and X to discover and evaluate athletes without needing to attend every event.
- Athlete SEO and platform profiles should focus on discoverability basics: real name, location, high school/club team, class year, GPA, sport-specific positions, highlight video, and a link to a recruiting profile.
- Recruit-friendly profile keywords include: student-athlete, sport, position, team, graduating year, hometown, highlight video, recruiting profile, GPA, training, camps, showcases, tournaments, and college coach.
- Content strategy should be humble, positive, and proof-based: game highlights, academic and athletic achievements, training progress, teammate support, overcoming adversity, and coachability.
- Cross-platform consistency matters. The same name, profile photo, athlete bio, and recruiting link should appear across social media profiles so coaches can identify athletes quickly.
- Backlink/internal-link opportunity: athlete profile pages should link to highlight videos, recruiting profiles, team pages, school pages, camp/showcase pages, and sport-specific content clusters.
- Compliance content opportunity: NCAA social media rules and the “click don’t type” concept create educational article opportunities around safe coach communication, recruiting timelines, and what athletes should/should not post.

## Preliminary SEO Implications

AthlynXAI should build keyword clusters around athlete discoverability, recruit-friendly profiles, social media for recruiting, NIL readiness, highlight videos, coach communication, camp/showcase exposure, and athlete brand building. The platform should treat every athlete profile as a searchable media asset that can connect talent, proof, social content, and next-step recruiting tasks.

## Full Stack Layer Cake Tokenization Requirement

The Auto SEO roadmap now includes **Full Stack Layer Cake Tokenization** as the operating model. Email, SMS, Calendar, CRM, Contacts, athlete profiles, social signals, SEO articles, backlinks, Trail evidence, and approval states should be represented as linked tokens rather than disconnected records. This lets AI agents and trainers work across iOS, Android, desktop, mobile web, laptop, Gmail, Google Calendar, Google Contacts, GitHub, Vercel, Neon, and Sentry without losing context or control.

The system must stay approval-gated. It may research, classify, draft, recommend, score, and create internal tasks automatically. It must not publish, send outreach, alter live metadata, buy links, modify production database state, or contact third parties without explicit approval.

## User Growth and Social CRM Linking Requirement

The current low-user state should be handled as a measurement and onboarding problem before it is treated as a marketing problem. If the active user base is roughly a dozen accounts and those accounts are mostly founder, test, partner, or internal records, AthlynXAI needs a user reality audit before reporting adoption. The CRM should classify internal/admin users, test users, partners, athletes, parents, coaches, brands, customers, duplicates, inactive accounts, and retired accounts as separate categories. This prevents vanity metrics and creates a clean baseline for real user growth.

The roadmap should also connect all owned and approved social identities to the AthlynXAI CRM and platform. That does not mean uncontrolled posting or private-data ingestion. The safe first step is to inventory handles, URLs, ownership, platform purpose, approval level, and the related CRM person/company/athlete/campaign. This turns social media into a controlled CRM graph that can later support Buffer scheduling, Zapier automation, Gravatar/profile completeness, Jira issue creation, Confluence playbooks, and onboarding-growth tasks.
